## The Foundation

The old covenant was glorious, but it was never meant to last. The prophets knew it. Jeremiah, writing as Jerusalem crumbled and the temple burned, delivered the most remarkable promise in the Old Testament: "Behold, the days are coming, declares the LORD, when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and the house of Judah, not like the covenant that I made with their fathers... I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts. And I will be their God, and they shall be my people" (Jeremiah 31:31-33).

The old covenant was written on stone. The new would be written on hearts. The old required an endless succession of priests and sacrifices. The new would be mediated by one priest and sealed by one sacrifice. The old could expose sin but could never remove it. The new would achieve what the old could only promise: "I will forgive their iniquity, and I will remember their sin no more."

Ezekiel added another dimension: God would give his people a new heart and a new spirit, replacing their heart of stone with a heart of flesh and putting his own Spirit within them (Ezekiel 36:26-27). The new covenant is not a stricter version of the old. It is a fundamentally different kind of relationship — one powered not by human effort but by the indwelling presence of God himself.

| Passage | Significance |
|---------|-------------|
| Jeremiah 31:31-34 | The new covenant promise — law on hearts, sins forgiven |
| Ezekiel 36:25-27 | A new heart, a new spirit, God's Spirit within |
| Ezekiel 37:1-14 | The valley of dry bones — resurrection and restoration |
| Isaiah 42:6 | The Servant as "a covenant for the people, a light for the nations" |

**In your study:** The Last Supper institution of the new covenant is in Week 17. The Hebrews argument for the new covenant's superiority spans Weeks 46-47. The pouring out of the Spirit at Pentecost is in Week 21. Paul's new covenant theology in 2 Corinthians is in Week 33.

## Looking Ahead

The new covenant is already in effect — every believer lives under its promises. But it is not yet fully realized. The Spirit is the "guarantee" (2 Corinthians 1:22), the down payment of what is to come. When Christ returns, the new covenant's deepest promise will be fulfilled without remainder: "They shall all know me, from the least of them to the greatest" (Jeremiah 31:34). No more teaching, no more striving, no more partial knowledge. Face to face, fully known, fully home.
