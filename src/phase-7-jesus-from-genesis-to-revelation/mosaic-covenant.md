## The Foundation

Four hundred years after Abraham, his descendants are slaves in Egypt. God raises up Moses, sends ten plagues against Pharaoh, and delivers Israel through the Red Sea — the defining act of salvation in the Old Testament. But the exodus is not just a rescue. It is a journey toward a covenant. At Mount Sinai, God offers Israel a relationship unlike anything the ancient world had seen: "You shall be to me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation" (Exodus 19:6). The law given at Sinai — moral, ceremonial, and civil — defines what it means to live as God's covenant people in a fallen world.

Three elements of the Mosaic covenant point directly to Christ. First, the Passover lamb — an unblemished animal whose blood on the doorposts spares Israel from the angel of death. Every Passover for fourteen centuries rehearsed this story, until the night when Jesus took the bread and the cup and said, "This is my body... this is my blood of the covenant." Second, the sacrificial system — the endless cycle of offerings at the tabernacle and later the temple, each one a reminder that sin requires blood and that no animal sacrifice could ultimately remove it. Third, the tabernacle itself — God's dwelling place in the midst of his people, the intersection of heaven and earth, the place where a holy God and a sinful people could meet through the mediation of priests, blood, and mercy.

The law was never the destination. Paul calls it a "guardian until Christ came" (Galatians 3:24). It revealed what holiness looks like and how far humanity falls short — and in doing so, it created the hunger that only Christ could satisfy.

## Key Old Testament Passages

| Passage | Significance |
|---------|-------------|
| Exodus 12:1-13 | The Passover lamb — blood on the doorposts |
| Exodus 19:3-6 | "A kingdom of priests and a holy nation" |
| Exodus 20:1-17 | The Ten Commandments |
| Exodus 25:8-9 | "Let them make me a sanctuary, that I may dwell in their midst" |

| Isaiah 53:7 | "Like a lamb that is led to the slaughter" |

**In your study:** The Passover connection runs through the Last Supper in Week 17 and the crucifixion in Week 19. The Hebrews argument about Christ's superior priesthood and sacrifice spans Weeks 45-47. Galatians' law-and-grace argument is in Week 25.

## Looking Ahead

The tabernacle was a temporary dwelling — a tent in the wilderness. The temple that replaced it was destroyed twice. But in Revelation 21:3, John hears a voice from the throne declare: "Behold, the dwelling place of God is with man. He will dwell with them, and they will be his people." No temple is needed in the new Jerusalem, "for its temple is the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb" (Revelation 21:22). The Mosaic covenant's deepest longing — God living among his people — finds its permanent fulfillment not in a building but in a person, and through that person, in a city that will never pass away.
