## The Foundation

Before there was a manger, before there was a cross, before there was a garden called Eden — there was the Word. John's Gospel opens by reaching behind Genesis 1:1 to declare that the agent of all creation is the same person who will one day walk the roads of Galilee. "All things were made through him, and without him was not any thing made that was made." The Christ of the New Testament is not a figure who appears midway through the story. He is there at the beginning, speaking light into darkness, forming the heavens, breathing life into dust.

The creation itself bears his signature. The order, the beauty, the goodness that God pronounces over each day's work — these are the handiwork of the one Paul will later call "the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of all creation" in whom "all things hold together" (Colossians 1:15-17). To read Genesis 1 after reading John 1 and Colossians 1 is to see creation as a Christological event. The world was made by him, for him, and through him.

## Key Old Testament Passages

| Passage | Significance |
|---------|-------------|
| Genesis 1:1-2:3 | God creates by speaking — the Word active in creation |
| Psalm 33:6 | "By the word of the LORD the heavens were made" |
| Proverbs 8:22-31 | Wisdom present at creation, rejoicing in the inhabited world |
| Isaiah 40:28 | The everlasting God, Creator of the ends of the earth |
| Psalm 104 | The Creator who sustains all living things |

## Fulfilled in Christ

| New Testament | Connection |
|---------------|------------|
| John 1:1-3 | The Word was with God, was God, made all things |
| Colossians 1:15-17 | All things created through him and for him; he holds all things together |
| Hebrews 1:2-3 | Through the Son, God created the world; he upholds the universe by the word of his power |
| 1 Corinthians 8:6 | One Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom all things exist |

**In your study:** Week 1 opens with John's prologue. Weeks 46-47 explore Hebrews' declaration that the Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact imprint of his nature.

## Looking Ahead

The Creator is also the Re-Creator. The one who spoke "Let there be light" will one day speak again: "Behold, I am making all things new" (Revelation 21:5). The new heaven and new earth are not a replacement for creation but its restoration — the original intention fulfilled at last, with God dwelling among his people in a world free from death, mourning, and pain.
