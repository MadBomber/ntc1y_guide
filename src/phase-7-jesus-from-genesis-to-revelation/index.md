*Reference — Outside the 52-Week Study*

## Overview

The New Testament does not begin at Matthew. It begins at Genesis.

Every page of the Old Testament is preparing for someone. A seed is promised in a garden. A ram takes a son's place on a mountain. A lamb's blood marks the doorposts of the enslaved. A tabernacle brings God's presence into the center of the camp. A prophet like Moses is foretold. A king after God's own heart receives an eternal throne. A suffering servant bears the sins of many. A son of man approaches the Ancient of Days and receives dominion over all nations.

This reference section traces the thread of Christ through the entire Bible — from the Word who spoke creation into existence to the Lamb who makes all things new. It is organized around the seven great covenants of Scripture, each one a promise that finds its ultimate fulfillment in Jesus of Nazareth. Within each covenant, the story moves chronologically: the Old Testament foundation, the New Testament fulfillment, and the forward look toward the Second Coming.

This is not part of the 52-week reading plan. It is a companion — a place to stand back and see the whole tapestry at once. When you encounter Christ in the weekly study, these pages will show you where he has been all along. And for those who want to go deeper, each covenant section sketches an outline that could one day become a full study of the Old Testament in its own right.

The covenants build on one another like movements in a symphony. Each one adds a new dimension to God's plan of redemption, and each one points more clearly to the one who will fulfill them all.
