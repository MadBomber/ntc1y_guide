## The Foundation

The story breaks almost immediately. Genesis 3 records the catastrophe that every other page of the Bible exists to remedy — humanity's rebellion against its Creator, the fracturing of every relationship that mattered, and exile from the presence of God. But buried in the curse pronounced on the serpent is the first whisper of the gospel: "I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and her offspring; he shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise his heel" (Genesis 3:15). Theologians call this the *protoevangelium* — the first gospel. A descendant of the woman will crush the serpent's head, though he himself will be wounded in the process.

From this moment forward, the Bible becomes a story about a seed. Who is the offspring? The question drives the narrative through Abel and Seth, through the genealogies, through the narrowing of the line until it arrives at one specific man in one specific family in one specific nation. Every genealogy in Scripture — those long lists of names that readers are tempted to skip — is tracking this promise. Matthew's Gospel opens with one. Luke's traces it all the way back to Adam. The seed promised in the garden is the child born in Bethlehem.

## Key Old Testament Passages

| Passage | Significance |
|---------|-------------|
| Genesis 3:15 | The protoevangelium — the seed of the woman will crush the serpent |
| Genesis 3:21 | God provides animal skins — the first blood shed to cover sin |
| Genesis 4:25-26 | Seth born as the line of promise after Abel's murder |
| Genesis 5 | The genealogy from Adam to Noah — tracing the seed |
| Isaiah 7:14 | "The virgin shall conceive and bear a son" |
| Isaiah 9:6-7 | "For to us a child is born, to us a son is given" |

## Fulfilled in Christ

| New Testament | Connection |
|---------------|------------|
| Galatians 4:4 | "When the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son, born of woman" |
| Romans 5:12-21 | Adam and Christ — the first man brought death, the last Adam brings life |
| 1 Corinthians 15:22, 45 | "As in Adam all die, so also in Christ shall all be made alive" |
| Matthew 1:1-17 | The genealogy tracing Jesus through David and Abraham back to the promise |
| Luke 3:23-38 | The genealogy tracing Jesus back to Adam, "the son of God" |
| Revelation 12:1-5 | The woman, the child, and the dragon — the cosmic fulfillment of Genesis 3:15 |

**In your study:** Week 1 covers both genealogies. Romans 5 (the Adam-Christ parallel) appears in Week 35. Revelation 12 appears in Week 52.

## Looking Ahead

The bruising of the heel happened at Calvary. The crushing of the head began at the empty tomb. But the final destruction of the serpent awaits the return of Christ, when Satan is thrown into the lake of fire (Revelation 20:10) and the curse of Genesis 3 is reversed once and for all. The tree of life, guarded by cherubim since Eden, reappears in the last chapter of the Bible, bearing fruit for the healing of the nations.
