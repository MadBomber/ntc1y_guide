## The Foundation

The Bible opens with a garden. It closes with a city. Between them stretches the entire drama of creation, fall, redemption, and restoration — and at every turning point stands the same person. The one who walked with Adam in the cool of the day is the one who will descend from heaven with a shout. The Lamb slain before the foundation of the world is the King who returns to claim what has always been his.

Every covenant in this reference section carries within it a forward-looking promise that has not yet been fulfilled. The seed of the woman has crushed the serpent's head, but the serpent has not yet been finally destroyed. The ark has carried God's people through judgment, but the final judgment is still ahead. Abraham's offspring have blessed the nations, but not every knee has bowed. The Passover Lamb has been sacrificed, but death still stalks the earth. The Davidic King reigns at the Father's right hand, but his enemies have not yet been made his footstool. The new covenant is in effect, but we still see through a glass darkly.

The consummation is the moment when every "not yet" becomes "now." It is not a departure from the story but its completion — the final movement of a symphony that has been building since the first note.

## The Full Circle

The story ends where it began — but better. In Eden, God walked with humanity in a garden. In the new Jerusalem, God dwells with humanity in a city where the gates never close and the light never fades. The tree of life, barred since Genesis 3, stands again beside the river of life, bearing twelve kinds of fruit, its leaves healing the nations. There is no more curse. No more death. No more tears.

Every covenant finds its consummation here. The creation is renewed. The serpent is destroyed. The ark of salvation has carried God's people through the final judgment. Abraham's family is an uncountable multitude from every nation. The law is written on every heart. The Son of David reigns forever. And God himself — Father, Son, and Spirit — dwells with his people face to face, world without end.

"Behold, I am making all things new."
